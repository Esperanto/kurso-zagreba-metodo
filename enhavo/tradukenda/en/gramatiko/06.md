# *-u* in Noun Clauses

The imperative ending *-u* is used not only in direct commands (as we have already seen) but also in clauses introduced by *ke* after verbs of commanding, requesting, advising and wishing. (These often correspond to English clauses with "to".)

- _Mi deziras, **ke** vi lern**u**._ – I want you to learn (literally: I want that you learn).
- _La patro insistas, **ke** mi ir**u**._ – Father insists that I go.

**💡 Memory aid:** a wish or command in the main clause pulls the verb of the *ke*-clause into the *-u* form — not *-as*.

# The Preposition *je*

*je* is a wildcard: it has no precise meaning of its own and is used wherever no other preposition fits exactly. Among other things, it is the usual word for "at" when telling the time:

- _**Je** kioma horo vi venos?_ – (At) what time will you come? (literally: at which-numbered hour?)
- _**Je** la kvina horo._ – At five o'clock.

**💡 Tip:** when no preposition seems to fit, *je* is the safe default.

# The Verb *farti*

mainly used in the greeting:

- _Kiel vi **fartas**?_ – How are you?

# The Suffix _**-et**_

makes diminutives (small, slight):

- _dom**et**o_ – a little house
- _bel**et**a_ – pretty
- _varm**et**a_ – tepid

# The Suffix _**-eg**_

makes augmentatives (big, intense):

- _libr**eg**o_ – tome
- _grand**eg**a_ – huge, very large
- _bel**eg**a_ – superb
- _bon**eg**a_ – excellent

**💡 Memory aid:** learn the pair together — *-et* shrinks (*dometo* = little house), *-eg* magnifies (*domego* = huge house).

# The Suffix _**-iĝ**_

means "to become", "to get":

- _riĉ**iĝ**i_ – to grow rich
- _trankvil**iĝ**i_ – to calm down, to become calm
- _resan**iĝ**i_ – to get well again, to recover
- _geedz**iĝ**i_ – to get married, to wed

**💡 Memory aid:** *-iĝ* = to become (a change the subject undergoes by itself). Its partner *-ig* ("to make something happen") follows in [lesson 8](/en/08/gramatiko/).

# Relative clauses with *kiu*

*Kiu* can connect a small clause to a noun. It becomes *kiun* when it is the object inside that small clause:

- *la amiko, kiu venis* – the friend who came
- *la letero, kiun mi sendis* – the letter that I sent

# Times and repetitions with *foj*

*Foj* means an occasion or a time. With numbers it makes useful adverbs:

- *unufoje* – once
- *trifoje* – three times
- *kelkfoje* – several times

# Familiar roots in longer words

You are expected to keep reading words made from familiar parts:

- *vespero* + *manĝi* → *vespermanĝi* – to eat supper
- *letero* + *kesto* → *leterkesto* – mailbox
- *hejmo* + *urbo* → *hejmurbo* – hometown
