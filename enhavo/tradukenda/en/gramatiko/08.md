# The Preposition *da*

*da* is used in expressions of weight, measure or quantity:

- _kilogramo **da** sukero_ – a kilo of sugar
- _glaso **da** akvo_ – a glass of water
- _multe **da** ideoj_ – a lot of ideas

**⚠️ Watch out:** after a quantity word, use *da*, not *de*. *glaso da akvo* = a glass of water.

# The Suffix _**-ig**_

This "causative" suffix means "to make, to cause to be":

- _bel**ig**i_ – to beautify (make beautiful)
- _grand**ig**i_ – to enlarge, to make bigger
- _san**ig**i_ – to cure, to heal
- _mort**ig**i_ – to kill (cause to die)

**⚠️ Watch out:** don't confuse *-ig* with *-iĝ* ([lesson 6](/en/06/gramatiko/)). *-ig* = to make something change (*beligi* – to make beautiful); *-iĝ* = to become (*beliĝi* – to grow beautiful).

# The Suffix _**-aĉ**_

shows the speaker's low opinion of the thing:

- _hund**aĉ**o_ – cur (a wretched dog)
- _knab**aĉ**o_ – brat
- _skrib**aĉ**i_ – to scribble

# Questions with a preposition plus *kiu*

A preposition can stand before *kiu*. The preposition shows the relation:

- *Al kiu vi skribis?* – To whom did you write?
- *Kun kiu vi parolis?* – With whom did you speak?
- *Kontraŭ kiu vi ludis?* – Against whom did you play?

# *Neni-* words

Words beginning with *neni-* already make the sentence negative. Do not add a second *ne*:

- *Mi vidas nenion.* – I see nothing.
- *Neniu venis.* – No one came.

# More word-building parts

Small words and roots you already know can reappear inside new words:

- *sub* + *skribi* → *subskribi* – to sign
- *super* + *-a* → *supera* – upper, superior
- *trans* + *iri* → *transiri* – to go across
- *de* + *veni* → *deveni* – to come from
- *for* + *kuri* → *forkuri* – to run away
