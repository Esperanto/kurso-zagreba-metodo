# The Suffix _**-an**_

a member of, someone belonging to:

- _klub**an**o_ – club member
- _amerik**an**o_ – American
- _samland**an**o_ – fellow-countryman
- _samide**an**o_ – fellow-supporter of an idea (in particular, a fellow Esperantist)

# The Suffix _**-ec**_

makes an abstract noun — a state or quality:

- _bel**ec**o_ – beauty
- _varm**ec**o_ – warmth
- _simpl**ec**o_ – simplicity

**💡 Memory aid:** *-ec* works like English "-ness" or "-ity":

- *bela* (beautiful) → *beleco* (beauty)

# The Suffix _**-uj**_

forms the name of a container, a country or a plant:

- _manĝ**uj**o_ – manger, food-trough
- _cindr**uj**o_ – ashtray
- _Franc**uj**o_ – France
- _Aŭstr**uj**o_ – Austria
- *pomo* (apple) → _pom**uj**o_ – apple tree

# *-ig* and *-iĝ* again

You have now seen both endings:

- *-ig* means "to make something become or happen"
- *-iĝ* means "to become or change by itself"

Compare:

- *sidi* – to sit / _sid**iĝ**i_ – to sit down, become seated
- *nova* – new / _nov**ig**i_ – to renew, to make new

# More familiar parts in compounds

The exercises also reuse familiar roots in longer words:

- *rilato* + *-a* → *rilata* – related
- *parto* + *tempo* + *-a* → *partatempa* – part-time
- *sama* + *lando* → *samlanda* – from the same country
- *flanko* + *-e* → *flanke* – at the side
