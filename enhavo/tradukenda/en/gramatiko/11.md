# The Suffix _**-il**_

forms words for a tool, implement or means:

- _tranĉ**il**o_ – knife
- _hak**il**o_ – axe
- _ŝlos**il**o_ – key
- _timig**il**o_ – scarecrow

**💡 Memory aid:** *-il* names the instrument for an action:

- *tranĉi* (to cut) → *tranĉilo* (knife)
- *ŝlosi* (to lock) → *ŝlosilo* (key)

# The Suffix _**-estr**_

*-estr* names the head or responsible person:

- _urb**estr**o_ – mayor
- _ŝip**estr**o_ – ship's captain

With *-ar*, it can name a leading group:

- _lernejestr**ar**o_ – school administration

# Combining suffixes

Known suffixes can appear together:

- *manĝi* → _manĝ**il**o_ – eating utensil
- *ŝlosilo* → _ŝlosil**ar**o_ – set of keys
- *sana* → _san**ig**i_ – to heal
- *sanigi* → _sanig**il**o_ – medicine, healing tool

# Time, side and point as word parts

Some exercise words are built from common roots. The last root usually carries the main meaning:

- *mezo* – middle
- *tago* – day
- *nokto* – night
- *flanko* – side
- *punkto* – point

With *-e*, these often become adverb-like expressions: when, where, or from which side:

- *tage* – by day, during the day
- *nokte* – at night
- *flanke* – at the side

Roots can also combine directly:

- *mezo* + *punkto* → *mezpunkto* – midpoint
