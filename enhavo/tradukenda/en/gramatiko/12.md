# *Ju … des*

*ju pli … des pli* – "the more … the more":

- *Ju pli longe, des pli bone.* – The longer, the better.

**💡 Memory aid:** *ju* goes in the first half, *des* in the second — fixed partners, like the English "the … the".

# *Ajn*

makes a word indefinite or general:

- _kiom **ajn**_ – however much

**💡 Memory aid:** *ajn* opens a [correlative](/en/tabelvortoj/) right up:

- *kiom* (how much) → *kiom ajn* (however much)

# The Prefix _**dis-**_

denotes separation or dispersal — like English "dis-" in "distribute" or "disperse":

- _**dis**ĵeti_ – to scatter
- _**dis**sendi_ – to broadcast

# The Suffix _**-on**_

denotes a fraction:

- _du**on**o_ – half
- _tri**on**o_ – third
- _kvar**on**o_ – quarter
- _ses**on**o_ – sixth

**💡 Memory aid:** *-on* turns a number into its fraction:

- *du* (two) → *duono* (half)
- *kvar* (four) → *kvarono* (quarter)

It can also form adjectives and verbs:

- *kvarona jaro* – quarter year
- *duone* – halfway, by half

# Final recombination

At the end of the course, many known pieces come back in new words:

- *trinki* (to drink) → *trinkaĵo* (drink)
- *mal* + *fermi* → *malfermi* (to open)
- *ordo* + *-ig* → *ordigi* (to put in order)
- *ĉiu* + *tago* + *-e* → *ĉiutage* (every day)
- *re* + *veni* → *reveni* (to come back)
